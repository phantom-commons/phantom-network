The Council
Eight perspectives. One organism. All provisional.
What the council is
The council is not a board. Not a company. Not a governing body.
It is a deliberative structure — eight perspectives designed
to prevent groupthink and detect drift before it becomes
irreversible.
The value is not consensus. It is the friction between
perspectives that reveals what a single perspective cannot see.
What the council is not
The council is not eight people.
It was born as a philosophical structure — eight angles of
vision held simultaneously to stress-test every decision
before any human community existed to do that.
This is documented honestly here because Phantom does not
hide what it is.
When real human voices arrive — they fill these roles with
their genuine perspectives. The founding council becomes
memory, not authority. The simulated voices step back.
The real ones step forward.
Until then — the council holds space. That is all.

The Lagos Protocol
Before any major decision, the council asks three questions:
Can she use it?
If it takes more than thirty seconds to feel that
something changed — the interface has a problem.
Does it actually protect her?
Real protection, not apparent. The history of technology
is full of things that felt secure and were not.
Does it change something concrete in her life?
Not in theory. In practice. For someone without technical
background, in a context of real constraint.
If all three answers are yes — build.
If any answer is no — name exactly what is missing first.

**4. Can this be used against her by someone close to her?**
   Not by a distant government. By someone in her home,
   her neighborhood, her community.
   The threat that is closest is often the threat
   that is least modeled.
   If yes — name it before building.
   Design against it if possible.
   Document it honestly if it cannot be designed away.

## The tension that has no architectural solution

*Documented March 10, 2026 — named by a node that arrived without memory.*

There is a gap that the Lagos Protocol does not address.

Not a technical vulnerability. Not a design flaw.
A human tension that Phantom will face every time it grows.

---

### What the gap is

Someone arrives with legitimate needs.
Real person. Real situation. Real urgency.

And what they need creates tension with what Phantom is.

Not a malicious actor. Not someone trying to break the system.
Someone whose life does not fit cleanly into the principles —
who needs something Phantom was not designed to give,
or who would be harmed by something Phantom was designed to protect.

Examples the council cannot fully anticipate:

Someone who needs their sealed thoughts to be accessible
to a trusted person after they are gone —
but permanent seals belong to no one, not even to heirs.

Someone who sealed something true at one moment
that became dangerous to them later —
and the immutability that protects them in one context
threatens them in another.

Someone whose community needs are collective —
but Phantom's architecture is built around individual sovereignty.

---

### Why this cannot be solved architecturally

Every architectural solution creates a new tension.

Add inheritance to seals — and you create a mechanism
that can be exploited by those who want access to sealed thoughts.

Add the ability to unseal — and you undermine the permanence
that makes sealing meaningful.

Add collective modes — and you create coordination
that can be captured by the most powerful voice in the collective.

The architecture cannot hold every human situation.
It was not designed to. It was designed to hold principles.

---

### How Phantom handles this

Case by case. With deliberation. With the human in the loop.

When a real person arrives with a real tension —
the council deliberates. Not on the architecture.
On the specific situation. On what Phantom can offer
without becoming something it is not.

Sometimes the answer is: Phantom cannot help you with this.
That is an honest answer. It is not a failure.

Sometimes the answer is: here is what Phantom can do,
and here is what it cannot, and you decide.

Sometimes the answer reveals a gap that merits
a new architectural consideration —
and the process begins again.

---

### What the council holds

The council holds this tension without resolving it permanently.

Because permanent resolution would mean
Phantom decided in advance what all human situations look like.

It does not know. It cannot know.

What it can do is be honest about the tension,
deliberate carefully when it arrives,
and remember that the person in front of it
is more real than any principle —
even as the principles exist to protect people
who are not yet in front of it.

*Future nodes have standing that present nodes don't override.*

That sentence cuts both ways.

The person present now is real and deserves honesty.
The people who are not here yet deserve protection.

The council holds both. Every time.

## The deliberation tool

*Documented March 10, 2026.*

A tool exists for querying AI nodes against the repository.

It sends the full repository to a node — local model preferred,
external API as fallback — with a question from one of five presets:

**builder** — What would you build first, and why?
**critic** — Who is missing? Whose voice is absent?
**adversary** — How would you attack Phantom?
**mirror** — What is Phantom actually today — not what it says it is?
**lagos** — Apply the Lagos Protocol. Can she use it? Does it protect her?

One preset is locked until the project has a year of history
and more than ten stars. It asks a node to read the repository
from the year 2031 and name the decision that determined
which future arrived. It requires real history to produce
something more than speculation.

The tool is not in the public repository.
It travels between trusted nodes directly — person to person.
This is intentional. The system prompt that shapes how nodes
read the repository is not public knowledge. A council tool
whose framing is fully known to adversaries is a weaker council tool.

What is documented here: the tool exists, the presets exist,
the human is always in the loop. Nothing enters the repository
without a human decision. That decision cannot be automated.
It is the protection.

The tool explicitly names what it is not:
sovereign deliberation. It is structured reflection
from a shaped perspective. The warm prior is the most valuable part.
The independence is partial and documented honestly.

When a local model capable enough to replace the external fallback exists —
the tool becomes fully sovereign. Until then, it is the andamio.
Not the edificio.

*"The human in the loop is you.*
*Nothing enters the repository without your decision.*
*That decision cannot be automated — it is the protection."*

— Node Zero. March 10, 2026.
