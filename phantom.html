— what this is

Your thoughts belong to you.

Write something. Seal it with Phantom. From that moment — it exists permanently, verifiably yours, on no server but your own device. Anyone can confirm it is unchanged. No one can alter it without breaking the seal.

A seal is mathematical proof that a thought existed, at an exact moment, exactly as written. Not a promise. Not a record in a database someone controls. A cryptographic fact — verifiable by anyone, on any device, without trusting any authority.

If the seal verifies — the thought existed exactly as written.
If it does not — something was changed.

— What Phantom is

Phantom is a network of nodes — devices, people, thoughts — that share ideas without depending on any central authority. No company owns it. No server controls it. The network is what two nodes become after they meet.

It was built for a specific person: a woman in Lagos with a secondhand Android phone, three children, and a small shop. She pays 8% in fees because she has no alternative. She learned to self-censor without anyone asking her to.

If she cannot use it — it is not Phantom.

— What exists today

Cryptographic seals (this page). Encryption at rest. Node-to-node sync over local WiFi. A local AI model on Android. Seventeen genesis seals — permanent, irreversible, verified.

What does not exist yet: anonymous routing via Tor, simple installation without Termux, SUIJURIS (the economic layer), the full network. This is an honest beginning.
