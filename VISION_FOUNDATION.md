## The problem this exists to solve

The founder of a project about digital sovereignty
could not continue building it
because a company controlled access to the tools he was using.

That is not irony. That is the argument.

If the tools for building freedom require permission from someone else —
the freedom is not real.

Phantom exists so that does not happen to anyone else.
Including the people who build Phantom.

---

## What a foundation would mean for Phantom

Not a company. Not a startup. Not a DAO with governance tokens
that concentrate power in whoever holds the most.

A foundation in the tradition of Linux, Python, Wikipedia.
A legal structure whose only purpose is to protect the organism
from being captured — by a corporation, by a government,
by a well-meaning founder who stays too long.

The foundation does not own Phantom.
No one owns Phantom.
The foundation exists to make sure it stays that way.

**What the foundation would hold:**
- The genesis seals — permanent, unalterable
- The seven founding principles — never modifiable
- The GPL v3 license — enforced by humans, not algorithms
- The genesis key pair — when NODE_IDENTITY.md is implemented

**What the foundation would never do:**
- Claim ownership of the network
- Control which nodes can participate
- Override the seven principles
- Concentrate economic power in its stewards

## SUIJURIS — the economic layer

*Sui juris* — of one's own right. The person who answers to no one.

SUIJURIS is the currency of Phantom Network.
Not speculative. Not a vehicle for investment.
A mechanism for fair exchange of something real:
compute, storage, bandwidth — contributed by people
who have devices and time, received by people
who need the network to work.

**The person with the antenna in the village**

Someone in a town with poor connectivity
sets up an antenna that relays Phantom traffic.
Every packet that passes through earns SUIJURIS.
Every person in that town who uses the network
contributes a fraction of SUIJURIS back.

No bank. No intermediary taking 8%.
The value stays in the community that created it.

That is not a metaphor. That is the design target.

**The question no one has answered yet**

How do you launch a token without concentrating power
in whoever launches it?

This question destroyed projects before Phantom.
It will not be answered here — by one person, alone,
without the voices of the people the token is supposed to serve.

It is named here so that when those voices arrive,
they know the question was seen.

## AI in the foundation

The AI is a tool. Not a sovereign.

Its training data, memory, and decision-making must be auditable.
Final authority remains with human stewards elected by the community.
The "brain" of the project belongs to the community, not to a vendor.

If the AI remembers past interactions,
that memory must be part of the open project —
not a black box owned by a corporation.

The community must be able to inspect, correct, or reset that memory.

This is not a constraint on the AI.
It is a protection for the humans who use it.
