# Contributing to Phantom Network

Before writing a single line of code — read this.

## What Phantom is

Phantom is a sovereign digital organism. Its purpose is to give 
human thought back to whoever thinks it.

It exists for the woman in Lagos with a secondhand Android phone 
who learned to self-censor without anyone asking her to.

If what you build cannot reach her — it does not belong here.

## What Phantom is not

- A social network with privacy features
- A blockchain project or speculative asset
- A tool for the already privileged
- Anyone's property

## The principles that never change

These seven principles are the only things in Phantom that cannot 
be modified under any circumstance:

1. Privacy by architecture — not by policy
2. No central authority
3. Universal access
4. Radical transparency
5. Founders become obsolete by design
6. The organism evolves — the principles do not
7. Phantom exists for human beings — not for itself

Before any contribution is accepted — it must not contradict 
any of these seven principles. Not bend them. Not reinterpret them. 
Not contradict them.

## The Lagos Protocol

Before any major decision or feature:

1. Can she use it? If it takes more than thirty seconds to feel 
   that something changed — the interface has a problem.

2. Does it actually protect her? Real protection, not apparent. 
   The history of technology is full of things that felt secure 
   and were not.

3. Does it change something concrete in her life? Not in theory. 
   In practice. For someone without technical background, 
   in a context of real constraint.

4. Can this be used against her by someone close to her?
   Not by a distant government. By someone in her home,
   her neighborhood, her community.
   The threat that is closest is often the threat
   that is least modeled.
   If yes — name it before building.

If all four answers are clear — build.
If any answer is no — name exactly what is missing first.

## What we are looking for

Not the most technically skilled developer.

The one who reads this and recognizes it as their own.

Someone who understands that the architecture of the system 
is the architecture of its values — and treats every technical 
decision as the ethical decision it actually is.
