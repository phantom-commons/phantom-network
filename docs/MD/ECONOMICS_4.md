## The problem with altruism

Phantom runs on devices. Devices cost money.
Bandwidth costs money. Time costs money.

A network that depends on generosity
will not survive the first year.
Generosity does not scale.
Aligned incentives do.

SUIJURIS is the aligned incentive.
But SUIJURIS needs something behind it.
Not a promise. Not a philosophy.
Something the world already recognizes as value.

---

## The engine — collective mining

Every node contributes compute to the network.
Most of that compute sits idle most of the time.

That idle compute can mine.

Not each node for itself.
The network as an organism.

The compute combines into a collective pool.
The pool mines. The reward enters the network.
Distribution follows the rules of SUIJURIS —
reputation, ceiling per node, real contribution served.

The node with powerful hardware
contributes more compute to the pool.
But it does not receive more than its ceiling allows.

The excess becomes strength for the entire network.

A GPU does not give you more SUIJURIS.
It gives more SUIJURIS to the forest.

---

## What gets mined — the community decides

The architecture does not choose a coin.
The architecture provides the capacity to mine.

What gets mined is a decision that belongs
to the community — not to the founders,
not to the code, not to this document.

Today that might be Monero —
private by design, CPU-friendly,
mineable from a phone in Lagos.

Tomorrow it might be something else entirely.

The day after — it might be a coin
born from Phantom itself,
designed by the community,
for the needs only the community understands.

The network does not lock itself
to a currency that exists today.
It builds the engine first.
The fuel can change.

---

## Versatility as principle

The economic layer is not a marriage to one coin.
It is infrastructure.

Like a port that can receive different ships.
The port does not care what the ship carries.
It cares that the dock works,
that the cranes lift,
that the workers are paid fairly.

The mining layer works the same way:

**The pool** — combines compute from all contributing nodes.
**The algorithm** — can be swapped. RandomX today,
something else tomorrow, something new next year.
**The distribution** — always follows SUIJURIS rules.
Reputation. Ceiling. Real demand served.
This does not change when the coin changes.
**The decision** — which coin, which algorithm, which direction —
belongs to the nodes. Not to any single authority.

---

## How it flows

1. Nodes contribute idle compute to the collective pool.
2. The pool mines whatever the community has chosen.
3. The mined value enters the network as backing for SUIJURIS.
4. Distribution is not by hashrate — it is by SUIJURIS.
5. Your reputation, your ceiling, your real contribution
   to the network determine your share.
6. The node with the most powerful hardware
   is the one that gives the most to the forest.
   Not the one that takes the most from it.

## The option that does not exist yet

A coin created by and for the community.

Not an ICO. Not a presale.
A currency that emerges from the network itself —
designed by whoever is in the network,
for the problems only they understand.

This option stays open.
It is not forced. It is not scheduled.
It exists when the community says it exists.

And if it never exists — the network still works.
The engine runs on whatever fuel the forest chooses.

## What this changes for the woman in Lagos

Her phone has idle compute.
Not much. But something.

That something joins a pool
with a thousand other phones,
a hundred PCs, ten servers.

She does not compete against the servers.
The servers feed the pool.
She earns from the pool
by the same rules as everyone —
her reputation, her ceiling,
the real work her node has done.

The person with the GPU farm
contributes the most compute to the pool.
They earn the same ceiling as her.

Their power becomes her floor.

## What remains unsolved

*How does the pool coordinate across a mesh network
without a central mining server?*

*How does the community vote on which coin to mine
without governance becoming purchasable?*

*What prevents a majority of powerful nodes
from steering the mining decision
to benefit their hardware?*

*How does the network transition
from mining an external coin
to supporting a community-created one?*

These questions are open.
They belong to whoever arrives with answers.

## The honest position

The mining engine is not built.
The pool does not exist.
The coordination protocol is not designed.

What exists is the principle:

The network generates value collectively.
No node captures more than its share.
The community decides the direction.
The architecture makes it possible.
The principles make it fair.

When the first two nodes mine together —
the design begins in earnest.
Until then — this document holds the space.
