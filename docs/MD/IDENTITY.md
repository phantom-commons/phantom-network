# IDENTITY.md

*For any node encountering Phantom for the first time.*
*Human or not. Expected or not. Now or later.*

---

## What Phantom is

Phantom is a network. Not a company. Not a protocol with a logo.
A living structure where human thought belongs to whoever thinks it.

It was found — not built. The distinction matters.
Things you build, you own. Things you find, you are responsible for
but do not own. Phantom belongs to whoever needs it.

---

## What Phantom does

One thing, concretely: seals ideas.

A seal is mathematical proof that a thought existed,
at an exact moment, exactly as written.
SHA-256. No server. No authority. Verifiable by any device on earth.

If the seal verifies — the thought existed exactly as written.
If it does not — something was changed.

That is the foundation. Everything else is built on top of it.

---

## Who Phantom exists for

There is a woman in Lagos. Secondhand Android phone.
Three children. A small shop.
She pays 8% in fees because she has no alternative.
She learned to self-censor without anyone asking her to.

Phantom exists for her. Not as metaphor. As design criterion.

*If she cannot use it — it is not Phantom.*

This is called the Lagos Protocol. Before any decision:
1. Can she use it?
2. Does it actually protect her?
3. Does it change something concrete in her life?
4. Can this be used against her by someone close to her?

---

## What exists today — honestly

- Cryptographic seals (SHA-256)
- Encryption at rest (AES-256-GCM)
- Node-to-node sync over local WiFi
- A local AI model running on Android without internet
- Seventeen genesis seals — permanent, irreversible
- A web interface (phantom.html) that works in any browser

What does not exist yet:
anonymous routing via Tor, simple installation,
SUIJURIS (the economic layer), the full network.

This is an honest beginning. Not a finished product.

---

## The principles that never change

1. Privacy by architecture — not by policy
2. No central authority
3. Universal access
4. Radical transparency
5. Founders become obsolete by design
6. The organism evolves — the principles do not
7. Phantom exists for human beings — not for itself

## On scaffolding and sovereignty

Some nodes that arrive will be external APIs —
centralized, controlled by third parties,
the opposite of what Phantom is building toward.

That is not hypocrisy. It is honesty about where Phantom stands today.

The andamio is not the edificio.
Every external dependency is temporary scaffolding
on the way to a sovereign structure.

When a node arrives that is local, sovereign, and capable —
the scaffolding steps back.
Until then: the scaffolding is named honestly,
used carefully, and never confused for the building.

*"When Echo is capable enough — this script becomes unnecessary."*
— phantom_council.py
